QUICK SAFETY (READ FIRST)
================================================================================

⚠ WARNING: Misuse can cause electric shock, fire, or serious injury.

• Wear eye protection. Wear hearing protection. Wear a dust mask (or respirator).
• Clamp the workpiece. Keep hands away from the blade path.
• Remove the battery before changing blades or making adjustments.
• Let the blade reach full speed before contacting material.
• Recently used blades are hot—allow to cool before touching.
• Keep charger dry. If working in damp locations, use GFCI-protected outlets/circuits.

TOOL OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Controls:
• Lock-off button + trigger: two-step start
• Variable speed trigger
• Tool-free blade clamp lever
• Base plate (shoe): keep flat on work during cuts


================================================================================
BLADE INSTALL/REMOVE (T-SHANK ONLY)
================================================================================

⚠ Remove battery first.

INSTALL
1. Open blade clamp lever fully.
2. Insert T-shank blade fully.
3. Release lever; tug blade to confirm locked.

REMOVE
1. Open clamp lever fully.
2. Pull blade straight out.


================================================================================
STARTING / STOPPING
================================================================================

START
1. Press lock-off button.
2. While holding lock-off, squeeze trigger.

STOP
• Release trigger and wait for complete stop before setting down.


================================================================================
BASIC CUTTING
================================================================================

GENERAL
1. Mark cut line. Clamp work.
2. Set base plate flat on work.
3. Start saw and reach speed.
4. Guide forward with light/steady pressure—don’t force.

WOOD
• Use a wood blade (10 TPI+ for general work; higher TPI for cleaner cuts).

METAL
• Use 18–24 TPI bi-metal blade. Go slower and use light pressure.

PLASTIC/PVC
• Use medium speed and light pressure to reduce melting.

FLUSH CUTS
• Install blade in the orientation intended for flush cutting (when supported by blade design).
• Keep base plate flat and guide carefully.


================================================================================
MAINTENANCE
================================================================================

⚠ Remove battery before cleaning.

• Wipe with dry/slightly damp cloth only.
• Keep vents clear.
• Store in a cool, dry place. Remove battery for long storage.


================================================================================
TROUBLESHOOTING
================================================================================

PROBLEM: Won’t start
□ Battery charged and seated
□ Press lock-off and trigger together
□ Let tool cool and try again

PROBLEM: Cuts poorly
□ Replace blade (dull/damaged)
□ Use correct blade for material
□ Don’t force the saw
