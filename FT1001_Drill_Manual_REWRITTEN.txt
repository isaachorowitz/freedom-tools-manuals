GENERAL POWER TOOL SAFETY
--------------------------

WORK AREA SAFETY
‚Ä¢ Keep your work area clean and well-lit. Cluttered or dark areas increase the 
  risk of accidents.
‚Ä¢ DO NOT operate power tools near flammable liquids, gases, or dust. Power 
  tools create sparks that can ignite fumes or dust.
‚Ä¢ Keep children and bystanders at least 10 feet away while operating this tool.

‚Ä¢ Even though this is a cordless tool, keep the charger away from rain and

‚Ä¢ Use the right tool for the job. Don't force a tool to do something it wasn't

‚Ä¢ Remove the battery before making adjustments, changing accessories, or

‚Ä¢ Store the drill out of reach of children. Never let anyone unfamiliar with

GETTING STARTED
================================================================================

CHARGING THE BATTERY (FIRST TIME USE)
--------------------------------------

Before using your drill for the first time, fully charge the battery:

1. Insert the battery pack into the charger stand. Make sure the + and - 
   terminals align correctly (correct polarity).

2. Plug the charger into a standard wall outlet (100-240V AC).

3. The indicator light will turn RED, showing the battery is charging.

4. When fully charged (3-5 hours), the indicator light will turn GREEN.

5. Unplug the charger from the wall outlet.

6. Remove the battery from the charger.

IMPORTANT CHARGING NOTES:
‚Ä¢ The battery reaches full capacity after 3-5 charge/discharge cycles.
‚Ä¢ The charger, adapter, and battery will feel warm during charging - this is 
  normal.
‚Ä¢ Always unplug the charger from the wall BEFORE disconnecting the battery.

BATTERY MAINTENANCE:
‚Ä¢ Recharge when you notice reduced performance. Don't continue using a weak 
  battery.
‚Ä¢ Recharge at least every 6 months, even if not in use.
‚Ä¢ DON'T overcharge. Remove the battery after 5 hours maximum.
‚Ä¢ DON'T recharge immediately after charging. Let the battery rest for at least 
  15 minutes.
‚Ä¢ If storing the drill for a long time, recharge the battery before use.
‚Ä¢ If charging multiple batteries, wait at least 15 minutes between charges.
‚Ä¢ NEVER leave a charging battery unattended.


INSTALLING THE BATTERY
-----------------------

1. Align the battery pack with the battery slot on the drill handle.
2. Slide the battery in until it clicks into place.
3. To remove: Press both battery release buttons and pull the battery out.


CHECKING BATTERY CHARGE LEVEL
------------------------------

Press the TEST button on the battery. The indicator lights show the charge level:
‚Ä¢ 3 lights = Fully charged
‚Ä¢ 2 lights = Medium charge
‚Ä¢ 1 light = Low charge - recharge soon
‚Ä¢ No lights = Dead - recharge now

OPERATING YOUR DRILL
================================================================================

INSTALLING DRILL BITS OR SCREWDRIVER BITS
------------------------------------------

1. Make sure the battery is removed.

2. Hold the back section of the chuck (the part closest to the drill body).

3. Rotate the front section of the chuck counterclockwise to open the jaws.

4. Insert the drill bit or screwdriver bit as far as possible into the chuck.
   ‚Ä¢ For screwdriver bits, use a bit holder/adapter
   ‚Ä¢ Choose the correct bit size to avoid damaging screws

5. Tighten the chuck by rotating the front section clockwise. Tighten firmly 
   by hand.

6. Check that the bit is secure by trying to pull it out.


SELECTING DRILL SPEED (1 OR 2)
-------------------------------

Your drill has two speed ranges:

‚Ä¢ SPEED 1 (LOW): 0-350 RPM
  Use for: Driving screws, drilling large holes, drilling metal

‚Ä¢ SPEED 2 (HIGH): 0-1300 RPM
  Use for: Drilling small holes in wood, drilling plastic

To change speeds: Slide the speed selector switch to position 1 or 2.

‚ö† ONLY change speeds when the drill is completely stopped!


SETTING THE TORQUE (CLUTCH)
----------------------------

The torque ring controls how much twisting force the drill applies. This 
prevents over-tightening screws and stripping screw heads.

Numbers 1-17: Use for driving screws
‚Ä¢ Start with 1 (lowest torque) and increase if needed
‚Ä¢ Higher numbers = more force
‚Ä¢ The clutch will "click" when the set torque is reached

Drill Symbol: Use for drilling holes
‚Ä¢ This setting provides maximum power
‚Ä¢ The clutch is disengaged


FORWARD AND REVERSE
-------------------

The forward/reverse switch controls rotation direction:

‚Ä¢ FORWARD (clockwise): For drilling holes and driving screws
‚Ä¢ REVERSE (counterclockwise): For removing screws and backing out jammed bits

The switch shows directional arrows:
‚Ä¢ ‚Üí = Forward
‚Ä¢ ‚Üê = Reverse

‚ö† ONLY change direction when the drill is completely stopped!


TURNING THE DRILL ON AND OFF
-----------------------------

TO START:
‚Ä¢ Squeeze the trigger

TO STOP:
‚Ä¢ Release the trigger

VARIABLE SPEED CONTROL:
‚Ä¢ Squeeze lightly = Slow speed
‚Ä¢ Squeeze harder = Faster speed

This gives you precise control for starting holes and driving screws.


LED WORK LIGHT
--------------

The LED work light automatically turns on when you squeeze the trigger. This 
illuminates your work area for better visibility.

USING YOUR DRILL
================================================================================

DRILLING HOLES
--------------

1. Select the correct drill bit for your material:
   ‚Ä¢ Wood: Brad point or twist drill bits
   ‚Ä¢ Metal: High-speed steel (HSS) twist bits
   ‚Ä¢ Masonry: Carbide-tipped masonry bits
   ‚Ä¢ Plastic: Standard twist bits

2. Mark your drilling location with a pencil or center punch.

3. Set the torque ring to the drill symbol position.

4. Select the appropriate speed:
   ‚Ä¢ Large holes or metal: Speed 1 (low)
   ‚Ä¢ Small holes or wood: Speed 2 (high)

5. Position the bit at the mark and squeeze the trigger slowly to start.

6. Apply steady, moderate pressure. Let the drill do the work - don't force it.

7. For deep holes, periodically pull the bit out to clear chips.

IF THE BIT GETS JAMMED:
‚Ä¢ Release the trigger immediately
‚Ä¢ Switch to reverse
‚Ä¢ Use low speed to back the bit out
‚Ä¢ Don't force it - this can damage the bit or drill


DRIVING SCREWS
--------------

1. Select the correct screwdriver bit for your screw type:
   ‚Ä¢ Phillips head: Use Phillips bit (#1, #2, or #3)
   ‚Ä¢ Flat head: Use flat blade bit
   ‚Ä¢ Square drive: Use Robertson bit
   ‚Ä¢ Star: Use Torx bit

2. Insert the bit into a bit holder/adapter, then into the chuck.

3. Set the torque ring to 1 (lowest setting).

4. Select Speed 1 (low speed).

5. Set the forward/reverse switch to forward (‚Üí).

6. Position the bit squarely in the screw head.

7. Hold the drill straight and apply steady pressure while squeezing the 
   trigger.

8. If the screw isn't fully seated and the clutch clicks, increase the torque 
   setting and try again.

TIPS FOR DRIVING SCREWS:
‚Ä¢ Pre-drill pilot holes in hardwood to prevent splitting
‚Ä¢ Keep the bit pressed firmly into the screw head to prevent stripping
‚Ä¢ Use steady, even pressure
‚Ä¢ Don't over-tighten - this can strip threads or break screw heads


REMOVING SCREWS
---------------

1. Insert the correct screwdriver bit.

2. Set the torque ring to a medium setting (8-12).

3. Set the forward/reverse switch to reverse (‚Üê).

4. Position the bit squarely in the screw head.

5. Hold the drill straight and apply firm pressure while squeezing the trigger.

6. If the screw is stuck, try:
   ‚Ä¢ Increasing the torque setting
   ‚Ä¢ Applying penetrating oil and waiting a few minutes
   ‚Ä¢ Using an impact driver instead

‚Ä¢ If the drill feels hot, stop and let it cool for 15 minutes.
‚Ä¢ Keep the ventilation slots clear of dust and debris.

‚Ä¢ Don't completely drain the battery before recharging.
‚Ä¢ Store batteries in a cool, dry place (50-70¬∞F is ideal).
‚Ä¢ Recharge every 6 months even if not in use.
‚Ä¢ Remove the battery if storing the drill for more than 30 days.

GETTING THE BEST RESULTS
‚Ä¢ Use sharp bits - dull bits require more force and drain the battery faster.
‚Ä¢ Match the bit to the material.
‚Ä¢ Start holes at slow speed for better accuracy.
‚Ä¢ Secure your workpiece with clamps - never hold it by hand.
‚Ä¢ Wear safety glasses every time you use the drill.

TROUBLESHOOTING
================================================================================

PROBLEM: Drill won't start
‚ñ° Check that battery is fully charged
‚ñ° Make sure battery is fully inserted and locked
‚ñ° Check that forward/reverse switch is fully engaged (not in middle position)
‚ñ° Try a different battery if available
‚ñ° Let the drill cool down if it's been used heavily

PROBLEM: Drill runs but has no power
‚ñ° Recharge the battery
‚ñ° Check that you're using the correct speed setting
‚ñ° Make sure the torque ring isn't set too low (use drill symbol for drilling)
‚ñ° Use a sharp bit - dull bits require more power

PROBLEM: Chuck won't hold bit securely
‚ñ° Make sure bit shank is clean and free of debris
‚ñ° Tighten chuck more firmly
‚ñ° Check that bit shank is straight (not bent)
‚ñ° Chuck may be worn - contact service center

PROBLEM: Battery won't charge
‚ñ° Check that charger is plugged into working outlet
‚ñ° Make sure battery is fully seated in charger
‚ñ° Clean battery and charger contacts with dry cloth
‚ñ° Let battery warm up if it's very cold (below 40¬∞F)
‚ñ° Battery may be defective - contact customer service

PROBLEM: Drill overheats
‚ñ° Let drill cool for 15 minutes
‚ñ° Don't use low speed for extended periods
‚ñ° Keep ventilation slots clear
‚ñ° Don't overload the drill - use appropriate bits and speeds

PROBLEM: LED light doesn't work
‚ñ° This is normal if battery is very low - recharge
‚ñ° LED may be burned out - contact service center
