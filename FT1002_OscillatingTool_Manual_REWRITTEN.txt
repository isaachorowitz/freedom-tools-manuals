OSCILLATING TOOL-SPECIFIC SAFETY

GETTING STARTED
================================================================================

CHARGING THE BATTERY (FIRST TIME USE)
--------------------------------------

Before using your tool for the first time, fully charge the battery:

1. Insert the battery pack into the charger stand. Make sure the + and - 
   terminals align correctly (correct polarity).

2. Plug the charger into a standard wall outlet (100-240V AC).

3. The indicator light will turn RED, showing the battery is charging.

4. When fully charged (3-5 hours), the indicator light will turn GREEN.

5. Unplug the charger from the wall outlet.

6. Remove the battery from the charger.

IMPORTANT CHARGING NOTES:
‚Ä¢ The battery reaches full capacity after 3-5 charge/discharge cycles.
‚Ä¢ The charger, adapter, and battery will feel warm during charging - this is 
  normal.
‚Ä¢ Always unplug the charger from the wall BEFORE disconnecting the battery 
  (fire hazard prevention).

BATTERY MAINTENANCE:
‚Ä¢ Recharge when you notice reduced performance. Don't continue using a weak 
  battery.
‚Ä¢ Recharge at least every 6 months, even if not in use.
‚Ä¢ DON'T overcharge. Remove the battery after 5 hours maximum.
‚Ä¢ DON'T recharge immediately after charging. Let the battery rest for at least 
  15 minutes.
‚Ä¢ If storing the tool for a long time, recharge the battery before use.
‚Ä¢ If charging multiple batteries, wait at least 15 minutes between charges.
‚Ä¢ NEVER leave a charging battery unattended.


INSTALLING THE BATTERY
-----------------------

1. Align the battery pack with the battery slot on the tool handle.
2. Slide the battery in until it clicks into place.
3. To remove: Press both battery release buttons and pull the battery out.

ACCESSORY INSTALLATION AND REMOVAL
================================================================================

‚ö† ALWAYS REMOVE THE BATTERY before installing or removing accessories!

‚ö† WEAR PROTECTIVE GLOVES - Accessories are sharp and may be hot after use.


INSTALLING ACCESSORIES
----------------------

Your Freedom Oscillating Multi-Tool uses a 12-position mounting system that 
allows you to attach accessories at 30-degree increments for optimal working 
angles.

1. Remove the battery from the tool.

2. Select the appropriate accessory for your task (see Accessory Guide below).

3. Align the holes in the accessory with the locating pins on the accessory 
   mounting plate.

4. Choose the desired angle (12 positions available, 30¬∞ apart).

5. Make sure the pins fully engage into the holes in the accessory.

6. Insert the hex bolt through the center hole.

7. Use the included hex wrench to tighten the bolt securely. Don't overtighten.

8. Tug on the accessory to make sure it's secure.


REMOVING ACCESSORIES
--------------------

1. Remove the battery from the tool.

2. Use the hex wrench to loosen and remove the hex bolt.

3. Pull the accessory straight off the mounting plate.

4. Store accessories safely in a dry place.


USING THE UNIVERSAL ADAPTER
----------------------------

The included universal adapter allows you to use most competitor oscillating 
tool accessories with your Freedom tool.

1. Install the adapter on the mounting plate using the hex bolt.

2. Attach the competitor accessory to the adapter according to its design.

3. Tighten securely.

Note: Some competitor accessories may not fit or perform optimally. Use Freedom 
accessories for best results.


INSTALLING SANDING SHEETS
--------------------------

Your tool uses hook-and-loop (Velcro-style) backed sandpaper that attaches 
directly to the sanding pad.

1. Remove the battery.

2. Install the sanding pad accessory on the mounting plate.

3. Remove dust from the sanding pad surface if necessary.

4. Peel the backing off the sandpaper.

5. Press the sandpaper firmly onto the sanding pad. Make sure it's centered 
   and fully adhered.

6. To remove: Simply peel off the old sandpaper.

SANDING PAD CARE:
‚Ä¢ The hook-and-loop surface will wear out over time
‚Ä¢ Replace the pad when it no longer holds sandpaper firmly
‚Ä¢ Don't apply excessive pressure during sanding - this accelerates pad wear
‚Ä¢ Keep the pad clean and free of debris

USING YOUR OSCILLATING MULTI-TOOL
================================================================================

SCRAPING
--------

APPLICATIONS:
‚Ä¢ Removing old paint and varnish
‚Ä¢ Scraping adhesives and caulk
‚Ä¢ Removing bonded carpeting
‚Ä¢ Cleaning surfaces
‚Ä¢ Removing grout

TECHNIQUE:

1. Install the appropriate scraper (rigid or flexible).

2. Select medium to high speed (positions 3-5).

3. Put on safety glasses, dust mask, and gloves.

4. Install the battery.

5. Turn on the tool and let it reach full speed.

6. Position the scraper at a flat angle to the surface (about 15-30 degrees).

7. Apply light, steady pressure. Let the oscillating motion do the work.

8. Work with the accessory moving away from your body.

9. The scraping action only occurs when pressure is applied.

TIPS:
‚Ä¢ Don't use excessive pressure - this can gouge or damage the underlying 
  surface
‚Ä¢ Keep both hands on the tool
‚Ä¢ Work in sections for large areas
‚Ä¢ Take breaks to let the tool cool
‚Ä¢ For stubborn material, use a heat gun first to soften it


SANDING
-------

APPLICATIONS:
‚Ä¢ Detail sanding in tight spaces
‚Ä¢ Corners and edges
‚Ä¢ Small to medium surfaces
‚Ä¢ Wood, metal, paint, varnish
‚Ä¢ Profiles and grooves
‚Ä¢ Hard-to-reach areas

TECHNIQUE:

1. Install the sanding pad accessory.

2. Attach the appropriate grit sandpaper to the pad.

3. Select the speed based on material:
   ‚Ä¢ Soft wood: Position 2-3
   ‚Ä¢ Hard wood: Position 3-4
   ‚Ä¢ Metal: Position 4-5
   ‚Ä¢ Paint removal: Position 4-5

4. Put on safety glasses and dust mask.

5. Secure the workpiece with clamps.

6. Turn on the tool and let it reach full speed.

7. Contact the work surface with the sanding pad.

8. Move the tool in long, steady strokes.

9. Overlap strokes by about 75%.

10. Don't apply excessive pressure - let the tool do the work.

11. For profiles and grooves, use the tip or edge of the sanding pad.

12. Rotate the accessory occasionally to distribute wear evenly.

‚Ä¢ Rough wood sanding
‚Ä¢ Rust or old finish removal
‚Ä¢ Heavy material removal

Fine (150-220 grit):
‚Ä¢ Final finishing of wood
‚Ä¢ Smoothing metal and plaster
‚Ä¢ Preparing surfaces for paint

Extra Fine (320+ grit):
‚Ä¢ Final sanding of bare wood
‚Ä¢ Smoothing old paint
‚Ä¢ Preparing finished surfaces for recoating

TIPS:
‚Ä¢ Start with coarser grit and work up to finer grits
‚Ä¢ Sand with the grain on wood
‚Ä¢ Keep the tool moving - don't stay in one spot
‚Ä¢ Don't apply excessive pressure
‚Ä¢ Change sandpaper when it becomes clogged or worn

SAWING AND CUTTING
------------------

APPLICATIONS:
‚Ä¢ Cutting wood, plastic, drywall
‚Ä¢ Cutting non-ferrous metals
‚Ä¢ Plunge cuts
‚Ä¢ Flush cuts against walls
‚Ä¢ Cutting fasteners (nails, screws)
‚Ä¢ Trimming door jambs
‚Ä¢ Cutting flooring

TECHNIQUE:

1. Install the appropriate saw blade for your material.

2. Select high speed (positions 5-6).

3. Put on safety glasses, hearing protection, dust mask, and gloves.

4. Mark your cut line clearly.

5. Secure the workpiece firmly with clamps.

6. Turn on the tool and let it reach full speed.

7. Position the blade at the start of the cut.

8. Apply steady, moderate pressure. Let the blade cut at its own pace.

9. Don't force the tool - this can damage the blade or motor.

10. For plunge cuts, start at a shallow angle and gradually increase pressure 
    as the blade enters the material.

FLUSH CUTTING:

GROUT REMOVAL
-------------

APPLICATIONS:
‚Ä¢ Removing grout between tiles for repair
‚Ä¢ Cleaning out old grout for regrouting

TECHNIQUE:

1. Install the HM-Riff segmented carbide blade.

2. Select high speed (position 5-6).

3. Put on safety glasses and dust mask (very dusty!).

4. Position the blade in the grout line.

5. Apply moderate pressure and guide the tool along the grout line.

6. Work carefully to avoid chipping the tiles.

7. Remove grout to a depth of about 1/8" to 1/4".

TIPS:
‚Ä¢ Work slowly and carefully
‚Ä¢ Don't apply excessive pressure near tile edges
‚Ä¢ Use a vacuum to collect dust as you work
‚Ä¢ Carbide blades last longer than metal blades for this application
