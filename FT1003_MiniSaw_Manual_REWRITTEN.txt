GENERAL POWER TOOL SAFETY
--------------------------

WORK AREA SAFETY
‚Ä¢ Keep your work area clean and well-lit. Cluttered or dark areas increase the 
  risk of accidents.
‚Ä¢ DO NOT operate power tools near flammable liquids, gases, or dust. Power 
  tools create sparks that can ignite fumes or dust.

‚Ä¢ Keep the charger away from rain and moisture.
‚Ä¢ Never use the charger if the cord or plug is damaged.
‚Ä¢ Keep the charging cord away from heat, oil, sharp edges, and moving parts.
‚Ä¢ If you must use an extension cord with the charger, use only cords rated for

‚Ä¢ Dress appropriately. Don't wear loose clothing or jewelry. Keep long hair

GETTING STARTED
================================================================================

CHARGING THE BATTERY (FIRST TIME USE)
--------------------------------------

Before using your saw for the first time, fully charge the battery:

1. Insert the battery pack into the charger stand. Make sure the + and - 
   terminals align correctly.

2. Plug the charger into a standard wall outlet (100-240V AC).

3. The indicator light will turn RED, showing the battery is charging.

4. When fully charged (3-5 hours), the indicator light will turn GREEN.

5. Unplug the charger from the wall outlet.

6. Remove the battery from the charger.

IMPORTANT CHARGING NOTES:
‚Ä¢ The battery reaches full capacity after 3-5 charge/discharge cycles.
‚Ä¢ The charger, adapter, and battery will feel warm during charging - this is 
  normal.
‚Ä¢ Always unplug the charger from the wall BEFORE disconnecting the battery 
  (fire hazard prevention).

BATTERY MAINTENANCE:
‚Ä¢ Recharge when you notice reduced performance. Don't continue using a weak 
  battery.
‚Ä¢ Recharge at least every 6 months, even if not in use.
‚Ä¢ DON'T overcharge. Remove the battery after 5 hours maximum.
‚Ä¢ DON'T recharge immediately after charging. Let the battery rest for at least 
  15 minutes.
‚Ä¢ If storing the saw for a long time, recharge the battery before use.
‚Ä¢ If charging multiple batteries, wait at least 15 minutes between charges.
‚Ä¢ NEVER leave a charging battery unattended.


INSTALLING THE BATTERY
-----------------------

1. Align the battery pack with the battery slot on the saw handle.
2. Slide the battery in until it clicks into place.
3. To remove: Press both battery release buttons and pull the battery out.


================================================================================
BLADE INSTALLATION AND REMOVAL
================================================================================

‚ö† CAUTION: Recently used blades may be HOT. Allow to cool before handling.

‚ö† ALWAYS REMOVE THE BATTERY before changing blades.


INSTALLING A BLADE
------------------

1. Remove the battery from the saw.

2. Turn the blade clamp release lever counterclockwise to its full open 
   position.

3. Insert the T-shank blade into the blade holder from the front. Push it in 
   as far as it will go.

4. Release the blade clamp release lever. It will automatically lock the blade 
   in place.

5. Tug on the blade to make sure it's secure.


REMOVING A BLADE
----------------

1. Remove the battery from the saw.

2. Turn the blade clamp release lever counterclockwise to its full open 
   position.

3. Pull the blade straight out from the front.

4. Release the blade clamp release lever.

‚ö† Dispose of worn or damaged blades safely. Wrap in cardboard or heavy paper 
before discarding.

WOOD CUTTING BLADES:

METAL CUTTING BLADES:

PLASTIC CUTTING BLADES:

Your Freedom Mini Saw can cut in two different orientations:


FORWARD MODE (Standard)

Blade teeth face FORWARD, away from the saw body.

Use for:
‚Ä¢ Most cutting applications

‚Ä¢ General purpose work

‚Ä¢ Reducing tear-out on top surface
‚Ä¢ Cutting close to obstacles

To switch modes: Simply remove the blade and reinstall it in the opposite

STARTING THE SAW
----------------

This saw has a two-step safety system to prevent accidental starting:

1. Press the LOCK-OFF BUTTON on either side of the handle.

2. While holding the lock-off button, squeeze the TRIGGER.

3. The saw will start running.

4. Once running, you can release the lock-off button. The saw will continue 
   running as long as you hold the trigger.

5. To stop: Release the trigger.

‚ö† There is NO lock-on feature. You must hold the trigger to keep the saw 
running.


VARIABLE SPEED CONTROL
----------------------

The trigger is variable speed:
‚Ä¢ Squeeze lightly = Slower cutting speed
‚Ä¢ Squeeze fully = Maximum cutting speed (2,100 SPM)

Use slower speeds for:
‚Ä¢ Starting cuts
‚Ä¢ Cutting metal
‚Ä¢ Cutting curves
‚Ä¢ Better control

Use maximum speed for:
‚Ä¢ Straight cuts in wood
‚Ä¢ Cutting plastic
‚Ä¢ Faster material removal

CUTTING TECHNIQUES
================================================================================

GENERAL CUTTING PROCEDURE
--------------------------

1. Mark your cut line clearly with a pencil.

2. Secure the workpiece firmly with clamps. Make sure the cut area overhangs 
   the work surface so the blade won't hit the table.

3. Install the appropriate blade for your material.

4. Put on safety glasses, dust mask, and hearing protection.

5. Install the battery.

6. Position the base plate firmly against the workpiece.

7. Align the blade with your cut line (don't touch the material yet).

8. Start the saw and let it reach full speed.

9. Slowly guide the saw forward into the material. Let the blade do the work - 
   don't force it.

10. Keep the base plate flat against the workpiece throughout the cut.

11. At the end of the cut, release the trigger before lifting the saw away.


CUTTING WOOD
------------

BLADE RECOMMENDATION: 10 TPI or higher, thin kerf blade

TECHNIQUE:
‚Ä¢ Secure the wood with clamps
‚Ä¢ Place the base plate firmly against the wood
‚Ä¢ Start the saw before touching the wood
‚Ä¢ Use steady, even pressure
‚Ä¢ Don't force the saw - let the blade cut at its own pace
‚Ä¢ For thick wood, use a slower feed rate

PREVENTING TEAR-OUT:
‚Ä¢ Cut with the "good" side facing down
‚Ä¢ Use a fine-tooth blade (14+ TPI)
‚Ä¢ Apply masking tape along the cut line
‚Ä¢ Use backward blade orientation for less tear-out on top

CUTTING CURVES:
‚Ä¢ Use a narrow blade designed for curves
‚Ä¢ Cut slowly and carefully
‚Ä¢ Don't twist the blade - make relief cuts if necessary


TREE TRIMMING AND PRUNING
--------------------------

BLADE RECOMMENDATION: 6-10 TPI wood cutting blade

TECHNIQUE:
‚Ä¢ Hold the base plate firmly against the branch
‚Ä¢ Cut as close to the trunk as possible to minimize branch movement
‚Ä¢ For thick branches, make a relief cut on the underside first to prevent 
  bark tearing
‚Ä¢ Be aware of branch tension - it may pinch the blade
‚Ä¢ Never cut branches above your head
‚Ä¢ Watch for falling branches

‚ö† SAFETY: Wear safety glasses, gloves, and a hard hat when trimming trees.


CUTTING METAL
-------------

BLADE RECOMMENDATION: 18-24 TPI bi-metal blade

TECHNIQUE:
‚Ä¢ Secure the metal firmly - it must not vibrate
‚Ä¢ Use a slower cutting speed
‚Ä¢ Apply light, steady pressure
‚Ä¢ Don't force the blade - this reduces blade life and can cause breakage
‚Ä¢ Let the blade cool periodically on long cuts

CUTTING THIN SHEET METAL:
‚Ä¢ Clamp thin plywood underneath the metal
‚Ä¢ This prevents vibration and tearing
‚Ä¢ Produces a cleaner cut

CUTTING METAL PIPE:
‚Ä¢ DON'T cut straight through like wood
‚Ä¢ Instead, cut around the circumference of the pipe
‚Ä¢ Only one section of the blade should contact the pipe at a time
‚Ä¢ This prevents blade pinching

LUBRICATION:
‚Ä¢ Apply a thin film of cutting oil along the cut line
‚Ä¢ This makes cutting easier and extends blade life
‚Ä¢ For aluminum, use kerosene instead of oil


CUTTING PLASTIC
---------------

BLADE RECOMMENDATION: 10-14 TPI blade

TECHNIQUE:
‚Ä¢ Secure the plastic firmly
‚Ä¢ Use medium speed
‚Ä¢ Apply light pressure
‚Ä¢ Cut slowly to prevent melting from friction heat
‚Ä¢ Let the blade cool if you notice melting

TYPES OF PLASTIC:
‚Ä¢ PVC pipe: Use wood-cutting blade, medium speed
‚Ä¢ Acrylic: Use fine-tooth blade, slow speed to prevent cracking
‚Ä¢ Polycarbonate: Use fine-tooth blade, slow speed


FLUSH CUTTING
-------------

For cutting close to walls, floors, or other surfaces:

1. Install the blade in BACKWARD MODE (teeth facing down).

2. Position the base plate flat against the surface.

3. Guide the saw carefully along the surface.

4. The blade will cut flush with the surface.

APPLICATIONS:
‚Ä¢ Cutting off door jambs for flooring installation
‚Ä¢ Trimming PVC pipes flush with walls
‚Ä¢ Cutting cable ducts against floors or ceilings
‚Ä¢ Removing protruding nails or screws

TROUBLESHOOTING
================================================================================

PROBLEM: Saw won't start
‚ñ° Check that battery is fully charged and installed correctly
‚ñ° Make sure you're pressing the lock-off button AND trigger simultaneously
‚ñ° Try a different battery if available
‚ñ° Check that the trigger moves freely
‚ñ° Let the saw cool down if it's been used heavily

PROBLEM: Saw runs but cuts poorly
‚ñ° Replace with a sharp blade - dull blades cut poorly
‚ñ° Use the correct blade for the material
‚ñ° Recharge the battery - low battery reduces power
‚ñ° Don't force the saw - let the blade cut at its own pace
‚ñ° Check that blade is installed correctly and fully seated

‚ñ° Make sure you're using T-shank blades (not U-shank)
‚ñ° Push the blade in as far as it will go before releasing the lever
‚ñ° Check that the blade clamp mechanism isn't damaged
‚ñ° Clean debris from the blade holder
‚ñ° Contact service center if problem persists

PROBLEM: Excessive vibration
‚ñ° Replace bent or damaged blade
‚ñ° Secure workpiece more firmly
‚ñ° Use correct blade for the material
‚ñ° Don't force the saw
‚ñ° Check that all screws are tight

PROBLEM: Blade breaks frequently
‚ñ° Don't force or twist the blade
‚ñ° Use correct blade for the material (metal blade for metal, etc.)
‚ñ° Support workpiece properly to prevent binding
‚ñ° Use bi-metal blades for cutting metal or nails
‚ñ° Reduce cutting pressure

PROBLEM: Battery drains quickly
‚ñ° Fully charge the battery
‚ñ° Battery may be old - consider replacement
‚ñ° Use sharp blades - dull blades drain battery faster
‚ñ° Don't force the saw
‚ñ° Let battery cool between charges

PROBLEM: Cut is not straight
‚ñ° Keep base plate flat against workpiece
‚ñ° Don't force or twist the saw
‚ñ° Use a straightedge guide for long cuts
‚ñ° Replace bent or damaged blade
‚ñ° Secure workpiece firmly

PROBLEM: Saw overheats
‚ñ° Let saw cool for 15 minutes
‚ñ° Don't force the saw - use less pressure
‚ñ° Use a sharp blade
‚ñ° Keep ventilation slots clear
‚ñ° Take breaks during heavy use
